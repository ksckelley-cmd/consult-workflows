Project Apple — Document Review
Updated March 12, 2026  ·  Confidential
Priority Concerns
1

Employee 12-month protection: Buyer must maintain all continuing employees' base salary, bonus opportunities, retirement/welfare benefits, and severance at no less than pre-Closing levels for 12 months. TNH has significant planned workforce reductions. This clause as written would block those reductions or expose Buyer to breach. This is a large-company standard clause incompatible with a venture-funded spinout. Flagged as a potential deal killer requiring negotiation or removal.

Deal Issue – RED FLAGOpenMIPA
2

Bioprinting is explicitly carved out from the Seller's restricted business. Bioprinting is in TNH's future plans. This carve-out allows Ricoh to compete directly in a market TNH intends to enter.

Concern – Competitive ThreatOpenNCA
3

Seller restriction scoped to FDA-cleared or FDA-approved devices only. Products in development or pre-clearance would arguably fall outside the restriction, creating a gap in coverage during the period when TNH is building out its pipeline.

Concern – Narrow non-competeOpenNCA
4

Ricoh use of deidentified Company data. Ricoh may create and use deidentified Recipient Data for other lawful purposes, including to develop or improve Ricoh's own products and services. Read in conjunction with the bioprinting carve-out above, Ricoh retains the ability to use operational data from the Company's business to develop competing products during the TSA period. Risk level is unresolved pending TNH direction.

Concern – Compounds competitive exposure created by NCA carve-outs; operational data is a proprietary asset that should not be available to Seller for competitive useOpenTSA
5

Ricoh unilateral termination of any service on 30 days' notice. Either party may terminate any individual service for convenience on 30 days' written notice. Ricoh can pull any service at will with minimal notice. For a spinout that depends on Ricoh's infrastructure, systems, and personnel to operate post-Closing, this gives Ricoh continuous leverage throughout the transition period.

Concern – Creates material service disruption risk given Buyer's operational dependency on Ricoh post-ClosingOpenTSA
Potential Ongoing Risks
6

Non-compete restricted to the USA only. Ricoh is a global company and faces no restrictions in international markets. This asymmetry may reduce the Company's exit value to a future buyer.

ConcernOpenNCA
7

The passive investment exception (10% ownership, no management) provides no practical constraint on Ricoh as a global corporation. Ricoh could acquire a minority stake in a competing U.S. 3D medical device company with no restriction. The concurrent right offered to TNH provides no equivalent leverage.

Concern – Competitive ThreatOpenNCA
8

D&O Indemnification: Buyer must indemnify pre-Closing Ricoh officers and members of the Company for 6 years post-Closing for actions taken in their capacity as such (excluding only illegal or fraudulent acts). TNH is obligated to defend and fund protection for Ricoh personnel for pre-Closing actions. Buyer also guarantees the Company's payment obligations under this provision.

Concern – Buyer should not bear Seller's pre-Closing indemnification obligationsOpenMIPA
9

No Sandbagging: Seller has no indemnification liability for any breach that Buyer had actual or constructive knowledge of prior to Closing. Gary and Ryan are former Ricoh executives with deep insider knowledge of the Company. Ricoh could argue that virtually any post-Closing indemnification claim is barred. Constructive knowledge standard must be removed; actual knowledge should be limited to what was formally disclosed in the Disclosure Schedules.

Concern – Substantially limits post-Closing indemnification rights given Buyer's insider knowledgeOpenMIPA
10

Recipient cooperation as condition precedent. The recipient's timely cooperation and decision-making are conditions precedent to the Seller's performance. Any delay or failure by Recipient proportionally excuses Seller from performance without liability. This clause provides Ricoh a standing defense to non-performance that is difficult to rebut.

Concern – Establishes a standing Seller defense against performance obligationsOpenTSA
11

Out-of-Pocket Cost passthrough with no cap. In addition to the (undefined) Fees, TNH must reimburse all Out-of-Pocket Costs, explicitly including post-Effective Date increases in third-party vendor fees, licensing charges, surcharges, and unexpected transitional costs. TNH bears the full risk of all post-Closing increases in Ricoh's vendor costs, with no ceiling and no right to pre-approval.

Concern – Exposes Buyer to unbudgeted cost increases with no approval rightOpenTSA
12

Post-Closing tax restrictions survive indefinitely. Buyer cannot amend pre-Closing tax returns, change elections, or take a broad range of tax actions affecting pre-Closing periods without Seller's written consent — with no time limit. Standard practice ties this to the applicable statute of limitations (3–7 years). The indefinite survival is non-standard and operationally restrictive, particularly given TNH's likely interest in pursuing R&D tax credit strategies that touch pre-Closing periods.

Concern – Requires close legal review; imposes material operational restrictions post-ClosingOpenMIPA
13

Suspension for non-payment; 10-day invoice dispute window. Ricoh may suspend any service for non-payment of undisputed amounts without being in breach. Recipient has 10 days from invoice receipt to dispute in writing; failure to dispute = deemed acceptance. With fees undefined in Exhibit A, invoice disputes are likely. The 10-day window is short and the suspension right gives Ricoh significant payment pressure leverage.

ConcernOpenTSA
