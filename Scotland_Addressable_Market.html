Scotland Is the Entry Point Into a 1,500-Company UK Market

330 addressable companies in Scotland. 1,500 across the UK. Glasgow is the beachhead.
The Glasgow–Edinburgh BioCorridor houses more than 380 life sciences companies—over a third of all Scottish life sciences enterprises and 50% of its MedTech companies. Glasgow is where Avio operates first.

The UK is the market—Scotland is where the firm operates first

The UK HealthTech sector spans 4,360 companies, 196,000 employees, and £48 billion in combined turnover. Paul Benton, Managing Director ABHI, places 2,500 of those companies at the stage and complexity where integrated regulatory and financial strategy is actionable. When constrained to device and combination device development, the addressable market is 1,500 companies across the Golden Triangle, the Midlands, and Scotland.

Scotland’s combined life sciences sector contributes £10.5 billion in turnover with 760 companies at a complex stage, and 330 within the addressable population. The Edinburgh–Glasgow corridor holds 380 of the 760 active life sciences ventures and ranks second nationally for new spinout firms outside the Golden Triangle.

Glasgow accounts for 50% of all of Scotland’s MedTech companies that map directly to Avio’s current regulatory capabilities and is the immediate beachhead. Edinburgh extends the pipeline through digital health, SaMD and a smaller number of devices. Dundee and Aberdeen are monitoring positions as drug candidates mature toward combination product classification.

Combination product demand is understated. The ~12 confirmed companies in the spinout cohort excludes the larger pipeline of device-only and drug-only companies that will require combination product regulatory navigation within 2–5 years—and combination product engagements are the highest-value work in the practice.

Combination device capability adds the corridor’s highest-value segment to the addressable market.

The 330-company addressable market is actionable today. Combination device capability extends it. The pipeline of future combination product clients is already taking shape in specific institutions across the corridor—companies that are 2–5 years from drug-device classification and are relationship-buildable now. Each location below represents a concentration of that demand.

Medicines Manufacturing Innovation Centre (MMIC), Renfrewshire

The MMIC accelerates lab-to-commercial scale-up for advanced medicines. Biologics and cell and gene therapies moving through this infrastructure increasingly encounter drug-device interface questions—delivery mechanism design, regulatory classification, and CMC integration. Companies exiting the MMIC with a commercially viable therapeutic will frequently discover they have a combination product. This facility is a client pipeline and a relationship worth building now.

Glasgow Precision Medicine—Queen Elizabeth University Hospital

The Clinical Innovation Zone and Precision Medicine Scotland Innovation Centre are running population-level pharmacogenomic trials at scale. Therapeutics emerging from pharmacogenomic research routinely require patient-specific delivery mechanisms—the precise point where drug-device combination products are created. The companies in this ecosystem are 2–5 years from that classification question. The time to engage them is now.

Software-interfacing drug delivery (Edinburgh)

Edinburgh’s 65% digital and AI orientation is not separate from the combination product story—it is part of it. MHRA, FDA, and EMA are all developing regulatory frameworks for software-driven combination products. Edinburgh’s digital health companies building on drug delivery adjacencies sit directly in this intersection, and the regulatory classification frameworks are still being written. Avio’s dual capability in SaMD and combination products is a structural advantage in this segment.
