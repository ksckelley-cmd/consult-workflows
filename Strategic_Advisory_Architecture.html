The Strategic Advisory Architecture for MedTech Consulting
Situation

Life science ventures entering global markets face interlocking regulatory, commercial, and capital decisions. A wrong sequence wastes months and millions.

Complication

Traditional hourly advisory rewards activity over impact. The advisor bills for volume. The client absorbs the risk—at the point where uncertainty is most expensive.

Question

Boards and investors do not buy consulting hours. They buy a specific future state for their organization. What does that future state look like?

Answer — So What

The architecture aligns the activities and services to answer the client’s actual questions. Sprints produce the Commercialization Roadmap that is anchored to the future state. Pricing is based on impact, not hours billed.

Delivering engagement value: foundations define outcome — sprints deliver progress — deliverables are actionable

The engagement architecture has three layers whether a finance or regulatory project, or a combined project. Foundations define the total scope and outcome of the client engagement. Sprints are the fixed-scope, milestone-driven phases within each foundation—individually priced against the decisions they support. Deliverables—cap tables, financial models, FDA submissions, board packages—are the tangible artifacts produced within each sprint. They are evidence of progress, not the unit of value. Sprints are the unit of commitment and pricing — each validates before the next begins

The advisory relationship is a structured progression, not an open-ended engagement. The client commits to the next sprint only after the previous one is validated. This discipline ensures that the most expensive commercial and clinical decisions are made only after strategic uncertainty has been systematically reduced. Pricing is set at the sprint level, anchored to the impact of the decision each sprint supports.

Pricing anchored to impact transfers profitability risk to the advisor

In an outcome-based model, pricing is decoupled from time and anchored to the impact of the decisions being made. Sprints are priced individually as packages. The total engagement value is the sum of the sprints within a foundation. Deliverables — cap tables, financial models, regulatory submissions, board packages — are produced within sprints but are not priced separately. They are the evidence, not the product.

This is a critical distinction. Pricing a deliverable commoditizes the work. Pricing a sprint values the milestone it achieves. A cap table built in isolation is a spreadsheet. A cap table built inside an Investor Readiness Sprint — alongside the financial model, the investor narrative, and the data room, all pointed at the same fundraising milestone — is worth what that milestone is worth to the client.

This structure shifts both parties from a vendor relationship to a strategic partnership, where the advisor is rewarded for impact rather than inefficiency. For life science ventures, a failure in go-to-market strategy or a flawed regulatory sequence leads to years of delay and millions in wasted capital. Fixed-price, outcome-based sprints provide a predictable cost for a high-value result — a direct alternative to the unpredictable burn of traditional consulting.
